## 1. Objective
The AI in Healthcare Hackathon 2026 challenges participants to design artificial intelligence
systems capable of analyzing medical biopsy images.
Participants will:
- Train AI models for image classification and image segmentation
- Work with real medical imaging datasets
- Apply deep learning techniques with image processing techniques for pre-processing if
applicable.
- Submit predictions generated by their models
- Demonstrate their approach and solution
The competition is designed to test technical capability, model design, and practical
implementation.

- Scoring Overview (Total: 100 points)
Phase 1 — Model Performance (70 points)
 Classification performance: 30 points
o Scored using accuracy on the test set
 Segmentation performance: 40 points
o Scored using mean IoU on the test set
## Example:
If a team achieves 83% classification accuracy and 0.65 mean IoU for segmentation:
Model Score = (0.83 × 30) + (0.65 × 40) = 24.9 + 26.0 = 50.9 out of 70
Teams with the top 10 model scores will be shortlisted for the presentation phase.
Phase 2 — Top 10 Presentation Evaluation (30 points)
Shortlisted teams will be scored on:
 User Interface Design: 10 points

 Error Handling: 5 points
 Innovation & Creativity: 5 points
 Presentation & Clarity: 10 points
## Example:
If a team scores 46.75 out of 70 in Phase 1 and 25 out of 30 in Phase 2:
Total Score = 46.75 + 25 = 71.75 out of 100
The final ranking is based on the combined score. The top three teams will receive prizes.

- Judging and Quality-Based Evaluation
In addition to automated performance evaluation, the judging panel will assess the overall
quality, robustness, and understanding of the proposed solution during the presentation stage.
During the Top 10 presentations, judges may evaluate:
## Technical Understanding
 The team’s understanding of the methods used
 Ability to explain the model design and workflow
 Ability to answer technical questions about implementation
## Solution Quality
 Soundness of the approach
 Proper handling of data and outputs
 Reliability and stability of the system
## System Demonstration
 Clarity and usability of the interface
 Correct functioning of the AI system during demonstration
 Ability to reproduce outputs using the trained model
Innovation and Engineering Quality
 Creative use of AI techniques
 Efficiency of the proposed pipeline
 Practical design considerations
Judges may ask technical questions related to the model, data processing, and implementation to
verify the authenticity and understanding of the solution.
Teams are expected to demonstrate clear knowledge of their approach and system design.
Failure to adequately explain the system may affect the final evaluation.

- Academic Integrity and Authenticity of
## Work
All submitted solutions must represent the genuine work and understanding of the participating
team.
Teams are expected to:
 Clearly understand the methods, models, and code used in their solution
 Be able to explain their implementation and design decisions during the presentation and
Q&A session
 Demonstrate how their system processes input data and generates outputs
During the evaluation stage, judges may ask technical questions about the model, data processing
pipeline, implementation details, and system design to verify the authenticity of the work.
Activities that may lead to disqualification:
 Submitting work that team members cannot explain or demonstrate
 Using solutions obtained from other teams or external participants
 Copying code or models without proper understanding
 Sharing models, outputs, or solutions with other teams
 Manipulating outputs or evaluation results
If the judging panel determines that a team cannot adequately explain their solution or
demonstrate understanding of their implementation, the team may be disqualified from the
competition.
